Tart Cherry Juice: The Science-Backed Recovery Fuel Pro Athletes Are Drinking
By
Seiji Ishii
Editor in Chief
A dark red recovery beverage is quietly gaining attention in elite circles. Here’s why tart cherry juice deserves a spot in your nutrition playbook.
Move over, chocolate milk. There’s a new recovery drink gaining serious traction in the athletic world, and it’s backed by impressive research.
Tart cherry juice
– specifically from Montmorency cherries – has emerged as a powerful tool for athletes looking to optimize recovery, reduce inflammation and improve sleep quality.
If you watched this year’s Tour, you might have noticed something interesting: as soon as riders crossed the finish line after grueling stages, many top cyclists could be seen reaching for bottles of deep red liquid. That wasn’t an energy drink – it was tart cherry juice, and these elite athletes weren’t drinking it by accident.
While the bright red juice might seem like just another trendy superfood, the science tells a different story. Multiple clinical studies have demonstrated that this ruby-colored beverage offers legitimate performance and recovery benefits that go far beyond its antioxidant content.
Tart Cherry Juice: The Inflammation Fighter
One of
tart cherry juice’s
most significant benefits lies in its ability to combat exercise-induced inflammation. A 2010 study¹ found that runners who consumed tart cherry juice for five days before, during, and 48 hours after a marathon experienced significantly less muscle pain and faster recovery compared to those drinking a placebo.
The magic lies in the cherries’ high concentration of anthocyanins
– the compounds that give the fruit its deep red color. These powerful antioxidants work similarly to NSAIDs but without the potential side effects. Research shows tart cherry juice can reduce inflammatory markers like C-reactive protein and interleukin-6, both of which spike after intense exercise.
Dr. Kerry Kuehl, who led several key studies² at Oregon Health & Science University, found that athletes drinking the juice showed a 12% reduction in muscle pain following exercise compared to those consuming a placebo. Importantly, this reduction didn’t come at the cost of adaptation – the body’s natural response to training stress that leads to improved fitness.
Accelerated Recovery and Reduced Muscle Damage
Beyond inflammation,
tart cherry juice
appears to minimize actual muscle damage from intense exercise. A 2016 study³ tracked cyclists during a three-day simulated race. Those consuming tart cherry juice maintained better power output and reported less muscle soreness throughout the event.
The researchers measured creatine kinase levels – a marker of muscle damage – and found significantly lower concentrations in the cherry juice group. This suggests that antioxidants in tart cherries may help protect muscle fibers from oxidative stress during prolonged or high-intensity exercise.
For strength athletes, research⁴ showed that participants who drank tart cherry juice for eight days surrounding an intensive weight training session recovered their strength 24% faster than the control group.
The Sleep Connection
One of
tart cherry juice’s
most unique benefits is its natural melatonin content. Montmorency cherries are one of the few food sources of this sleep-regulating hormone, containing approximately 13.5 nanograms per gram of fruit.
A landmark study⁵ found that participants who consumed tart cherry juice twice daily for two weeks increased their sleep time by an average of 84 minutes per night. They also reported better sleep quality and spent less time lying awake.
For athletes, quality sleep is non-negotiable. It’s during deep sleep that growth hormone peaks, muscle protein synthesis occurs, and memories consolidate. The natural melatonin in tart cherry juice may help athletes fall asleep faster and achieve more restorative sleep – crucial for both recovery and performance.
Timing and Dosage: What the Research Shows
Most studies showing positive effects used specific protocols for optimal results. The effective dose is 240 ml (about 8 ounces) of 100% tart cherry juice or its concentrate equivalent, consumed twice daily.
For inflammation and recovery benefits, research suggests starting supplementation
4-5 days before
intense training or competition and continuing for
2-3 days afterward
. For
sleep benefits
, consuming 8 ounces about 30 minutes before bedtime appears most effective.
Not all cherry juices are created equal. Studies specifically used Montmorency tart cherry juice, which contains higher levels of anthocyanins compared to sweet cherry varieties. When shopping, look for 100% tart cherry juice or concentrate made from Montmorency cherries.
Considerations and Limitations
While the research is promising, tart cherry juice isn’t a magic bullet. It works best as part of a comprehensive recovery strategy that includes proper nutrition, hydration, sleep, and stress management.
Athletes should be aware that tart cherry juice is relatively high in natural sugars—about 25 grams per 8-ounce serving. While this can be beneficial for post-workout glycogen replenishment, those monitoring sugar intake should factor this into their daily totals.
Some medications may interact with the natural compounds in cherries. Athletes taking blood thinners or sleep medications should consult with their healthcare provider before adding tart cherry juice to their routine.
The Bottom Line on Tart Cherry Juice
The research on
tart cherry juice
presents a compelling case for its inclusion in an athlete’s nutrition arsenal. With studies showing reduced inflammation, faster recovery, less muscle damage and improved sleep quality, it offers multiple pathways to enhanced performance.
Unlike many sports supplements that rely on single studies or questionable research, tart cherry juice benefits are supported by multiple peer-reviewed studies across different athletic populations. The fact that it’s a whole food source rather than an isolated compound makes it appealing for athletes seeking natural performance aids.
While more research is always welcome, the current evidence suggests that this ruby-red juice deserves its growing reputation as a legitimate recovery tool. For athletes looking to optimize their training adaptations and minimize downtime, tart cherry juice offers a science-backed strategy that’s both natural and effective.
